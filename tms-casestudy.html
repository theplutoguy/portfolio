Simplifying the Indent Acceptance and
Vehicle Assignment for Transporters

B2B Saas Web & Mobile
Role
Product Designer
Period
Sept – Oct 2025
Product
Web & Mobile

Context

The Transportation Management System (TMS) is a logistics solution used by enterprise logistics teams and the transporter partners to plan and execute shipments. I led the 0 → 1 feature research and design (no prior MVP existed) on the TMS platform for Enterprise logistics teams and Transporter partners for indent acceptance and vehicle assignment. The solution reduced the dispatch delays and removed the support dependency for operational updates.

Problem Statement

The challenge was to formalise a non-trivial operational activity – confirming vehicle availability and preparing for shipment. The Enterprises could request multiple vehicles in single indent while the transporters might only have partial fleet availability. The system should support those operational realities without disrupting the shipment planning or dispatch timelines.

Objective

Design a clear and reliable workflow that allows transporters to respond to indents, allocate vehicles efficiently, and ensure the vehicles reach the right sites for loading without operational delays.

Design Decisions

I created the flow into a step-by-step layout that clearly separates the actions to be performed by transporters into acceptance / rejection of the indent and assigning the vehicles to accepted indents. I've designed these interactions carefully since this is the most critical point for users in the flow.

ADDITIONAL FEATURES
  • To support operational realities, I introduced Partial Acceptance, allowing transporters to fulfil only the vehicles as their availability.
  • For high-volume cases, I also added Bulk Upload option, enabling transporters to upload multiple assignments at once.

Post MVP Iteration with Redefined Solution

After launching the MVP to our early customers, my team and I started receiving frequent requests to modify driver and vehicle details such as updating driver names, vehicle numbers, or vehicle registration numbers. To understand the issue better, I got on calls with our customers to understand the issue better.

GATHERED FEEDBACKS
  • Users could change or the last moment.
  • Wrong vehicles sometimes linked to the vehicle tracking.
  • Different vehicles were substituted for loading error.
  • Swapping of incorrect entries made by Transporters.

Additionally, the customers wanted Save on 1-Save out functionality so best to remove the dependency on mobile devices.

Refined Solution

To address the above key feedbacks, I redesigned the system with the following updates:

  • Allowed vehicle details edits till Vehicle Out event.
  • Extended Vehicle Save on / Save out functionality on web platform (for now).
  • Added VAHAN verification to validate truck documents.

As part of this update, I also redesigned the Vehicle Details section on the enterprise side. It now provides access to vehicle documents and serves as a entry point to edit and manage vehicle information.

Impact

The redesign produced measurable operational improvements:

~95%
Removed dependency on supporting teams for updating details
~60%
Reduced the turnaround time between change request and trip movement

Key Learnings

Logistics workflows are always dynamic and unpredictable.

The simplest designs hold a lot of complexity:

Seemingly workflows to decouple this complexity and bring simplicity to users. Elegant solutions require a lot of exploration and iteration.

Post-MVP feedback is a part of the design process:

Post-launch revealed edge cases that could not be predicted during planning. The redesign came from observing real usage, not assumptions.
